[CLAIM]
The test of the selected claim is based on an interaction effect between position and the share of downloaded files from previous round (t - 1). A statistically significant and positive estimate means that if downstream participants get a higher share, they invest more in the shared infrastructure. Janssen et al. (2011) found the interaction effect to be statistically significant and positive for the 5-player game with six groups (third column, table 8) - unstandardized coefficient = 3.836, SE = .836, p < .01. 

[HYPOTHESIS]
In a five-player public infrastructure investment game, tail-ender subjects will increase investments in public infrastructure (dependent variable) provision under the condition that they receive a fair share of the resulting resource flows from previous rounds of the game.
